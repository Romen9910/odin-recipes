Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof Rice is a loved West African dish known for its rich, savory flavor and vibrant red color. The dish is made by cooking rice in a flavorful tomato sauce with onions, garlic, and blend of aromatics spices. Often served with grilled meats, fried plantains, or vegetables. Jollof Rice is a staple at parties and gatherings across the continent.

Ingredients

  • Long-grain rice (or any rice of your choice)
  • Onions
  • vegetable oil
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Red bell pepper
  • Garlic
  • Scotch bonnet pepper
  • Bay leaves
  • Thyme (dried or fresh)
  • Curry powder
  • Chicken stock
  • Salt
  • Ground black pepper

Optional Add-ins:

  • Carrots
  • Green peas
  • Chicken or Beef
  • Fried plantains
  • Bell peppers (green, yellow, or red)
  • Seasoning cubes

Steps

  1. Blend The Pepper Mix
    • Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and onions until smooth.
    • Pour the mixture into a pot and boil until it thickens.

  2. Prepare the base (tomato paste)
    • Heat vegetable oil in a pot over medium heat
    • Add chopped onions and stir until soft.
    • Stir in Tomato paste and fry for a few minutes to remove its sour taste.
    • Add the boiled pepper mix and cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the oil separates from the sauce.

  3. Add Seasoning and Stock
    • Season the sauce with salt, thyme, curry powder, bay leaves and stock cubes.
    • Pour in the Chicken or vegetable stock and stir.

  4. Add The Rice
    • Wash the parboiled rice thoroughly to remove excess starch.
    • Add the washed rice into the
    • Reduce the heat to low, cover with foil or parchment paper (to trap steam), and place the pot lid on.
    • Let it cook for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally and adding small amounts of water if needed.

  5. Final Touches
    • Once the rice is soft and has absorbed the flavor, add sliced bell peppers, carrots, or peas.
    • Stir everything together and let it simmer for a few minutes.

  6. Serve and Enjoy!
    • Remove from heat, discard the bay leaves, and fluff the rice with a fork.
    • Serve with grilled Chicken, beef, fried plantains, or salad.

Now, you have a delicious pot of Nigerian jollof Rice ready to enjoy!
